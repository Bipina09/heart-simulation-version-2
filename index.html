The Human Heart

The heart is a powerful muscle about the size of your fist. It beats around 100,000 times a day, pumping blood to every part of your body. Click any part of the 3D heart to learn more!

🔄 Blood Flow Journey

1
Body returns blood

Oxygen-poor (used) blood flows back from the body through two large veins called the Superior & Inferior Vena Cava

2
Right Atrium fills

Blood enters the upper-right chamber. The Tricuspid Valve then opens to let it through

3
Right Ventricle pumps

The lower-right chamber squeezes blood through the Pulmonary Valve to the lungs

4
Lungs add oxygen

Blood picks up fresh oxygen and drops off carbon dioxide. It turns bright red!

5
Left Atrium receives

Oxygen-rich blood returns via Pulmonary Veins. The Mitral Valve lets it into the left ventricle

6
Left Ventricle — main pump

The strongest chamber! Squeezes hard to push blood through the Aortic Valve into the Aorta (the body's biggest artery)

7
Blood reaches the body

Fresh oxygen-rich blood is delivered to muscles, organs, and your brain. The cycle repeats!

📚 Quick Facts

❤ The heart beats ~100,000 times every day
🩸 It pumps about 5 litres of blood per minute
💪 The left ventricle wall is 3× thicker than the right
⚡ Each heartbeat is triggered by an electrical signal
🫁 Blood takes ~60 seconds to complete one full circuit
🏃 During exercise, output can jump to 25 L/min!
